History of Myanmar Cuisine

About Myanmar cuisine

Burmese cuisine are inspired by India, Thailand, and China. What makes Burmese dishes distinct from Indian, Thai and Chinese cuisine is the traditional cooking techniques that vary a bit from one region to another. Apart from those cuisine derived from neighboring countries, there are also authentic traditional dishes and distinct dishes from minority ethnic groups.

Popular Dishes

Mohinga

Mohinga, a fished-based soup with rice vermicelli, is a famous breakfast of choice in Myanmar.

Ohn-no kauk swé

Ohn-no kauk swé, is coconut cream based soup and usually cooked with chicken.

Shwe-Yin-Aye

Shwe-Yin-Aye is a seasonal dessert of the month of April when Myanmar New Year is celebrated.
